This combination of 5 images creates a point of reflection. It contributes to the broader collage conversations frustrations and anxiety which often develop develop in people with invisible disabilities. It also attempts to start clarifying one-sided conversations that perpetuate stigma, and delusional thought patterns to develop as a result of miscommunications. This object also tries to, in a measured way deflect consequences of these one sided conversations that are always detrimental to all parties involved at some level. It advocates for constructive action in situations that usually trigger deep anxiety and depression in those who are marginalized by disability. Lastly owing to this machine's usual operating control mode(i.e. remote controlled operation) it contributes wisdom to the given interactions where it is present. That is, the operator standing outside of the picture can see the whole situation and attempt to avoid making a mistake. These situations can be both physical and communications based at the same time, or be just one aspect. This aspect is one hopeful feature in the otherwise grave nature of the overall negative experiences this point of reflection depicts.
The three images of myself at the three points of the triangle represent three random people who are engaged in a conversation that uses social norms that make it clear to me they are not looking to help me. My reflection sits in the middle on the bottom next to some teeth from a piece of road construction equipment. These components contribute to the communications aspects of the roots to the depression and anxiety that can make unwelcome contributions to my life. Further, they help to demonstrate how social situations can alienate people with invisible disabilities from families, friends and whole communities.
